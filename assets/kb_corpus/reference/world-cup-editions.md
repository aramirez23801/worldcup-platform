# World Cup editions (1930 to 2022)

Host, winner and runner-up for each FIFA World Cup.

- 1930 (Uruguay): winner Uruguay, runner-up Argentina.
- 1934 (Italy): winner Italy, runner-up Czechoslovakia.
- 1938 (France): winner Italy, runner-up Hungary.
- 1950 (Brazil): winner Uruguay, runner-up Brazil.
- 1954 (Switzerland): winner Germany, runner-up Hungary.
- 1958 (Sweden): winner Brazil, runner-up Sweden.
- 1962 (Chile): winner Brazil, runner-up Czechoslovakia.
- 1966 (England): winner England, runner-up Germany.
- 1970 (Mexico): winner Brazil, runner-up Italy.
- 1974 (Germany): winner Germany, runner-up Netherlands.
- 1978 (Argentina): winner Argentina, runner-up Netherlands.
- 1982 (Spain): winner Italy, runner-up Germany.
- 1986 (Mexico): winner Argentina, runner-up Germany.
- 1990 (Italy): winner Germany, runner-up Argentina.
- 1994 (United States): winner Brazil, runner-up Italy.
- 1998 (France): winner France, runner-up Brazil.
- 2002 (South Korea and Japan): winner Brazil, runner-up Germany.
- 2006 (Germany): winner Italy, runner-up France.
- 2010 (South Africa): winner Spain, runner-up Netherlands.
- 2014 (Brazil): winner Germany, runner-up Argentina.
- 2018 (Russia): winner France, runner-up Croatia.
- 2022 (Qatar): winner Argentina, runner-up France.

## Title counts
- Brazil: 5 (1958, 1962, 1970, 1994, 2002)
- Italy: 4 (1934, 1938, 1982, 2006)
- Germany: 4 (1954, 1974, 1990, 2014) (the first three as West Germany)
- Argentina: 3 (1978, 1986, 2022)
- Uruguay: 2 (1930, 1950)
- France: 2 (1998, 2018)
- England: 1 (1966)
